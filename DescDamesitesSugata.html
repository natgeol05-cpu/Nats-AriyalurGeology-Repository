Name: Damesites sugata

Classification ; Kingdom: Animalia, Phylum: Mollusca, Class: Cephalopoda, Order: Ammonitida, Superfamily:
Desmoceratoidea, Family: Desmoceratidae, and Genus: Paradamesites.

Damesites sugata (Forbes, 1846) is an extinct ammonite species from the Late Cretaceous period,
classified within the family Desmoceratidae. Initially described as Ammonites sugata by Edward Forbes in 1846,
fossils have been found in deposits from approximately 87 to 83 million years ago across various global
locations, including South India and South Africa.

While historically placed in the genus Damesites, recent studies have reclassified it as Paradamesites sugata.
Recent research has moved this species from the genus Damesites to the new genus Paradamesites.
The new combination, Paradamesites sugata, is now the accepted scientific name.
It was previously known by the synonym Ammonites sugata (Forbes, 1846).

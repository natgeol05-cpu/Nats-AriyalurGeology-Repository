Marsupites is an extinct genus of crinoids from the Santonian stage of the Late Cretaceous.

Features: Cup or ballon-like body with hexagonal plates.

Biostratigraphic significance:
The International Commission on Stratigraphy (ICS) had provisionally assigned the extinction of Marsupites testudinarius
as the defining biological marker for the start of the Campanian stage of the Late Cretaceous, 83.6 ± 0.2 million years ago,
until a new final definition was eventually ratified in 2022, based instead primarily on magnetostratigraphy.

Distribution:
Fossils of the genus have been found in:
• Lipnik, Poland
• Haslam Formation, British Columbia, Canada

Pachydesmoceras pachydiscoide is a species of ammonite, a type of extinct marine mollusk,
from the Cretaceous period, first described by E. Matsumoto in 1954.
It is a member of the genus Pachydesmoceras, belonging to the family Desmoceratidae.
This species is characterized by its large size and specific shell morphology, including early conchs
with requent constrictions that become less distinct in later stages, and a nearly smooth mature shell.
Features: Giant ammonite (more than 2 feet diameter) with prominent ribbing developing into folds in body chamber area.

• Taxonomy: Pachydesmoceras pachydiscoide is an extinct species of ammonite.
• Classification: It belongs to the genus Pachydesmoceras and the family Desmoceratidae.
• Author and Date: The species was named after T. Matsumoto - Tasuro Matsumoto in 1954. • Geological Period: It lived during the Cretaceous period.

• Morphology:
The early conch features frequent constrictions.
In later growth stages, the shell becomes smoother with weaker ribs.
It is known for its gigantic size.

• Fossil Record:
Fossils have been found in India, Tamil Nadu.
The genus has a wider distribution, with other species, such as Pachydesmoceras denisonianum,
also described from locations like India.
Fossils have also been reported from other regions, including Poland,
where specimens have been tentatively identified as P. cf. pachydiscoide.

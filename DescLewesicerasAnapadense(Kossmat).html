Description

Name: Lewesiceras anapadense (Kossmat)
Formation: Silakkudi, Anaipadi, Kulakkanatham and Karai Formations
Locality: Silakkudi, Anaipadi, Kulakkanatham and Karai
Classification ; Kingdom: Animalia, Phylum: Mollusca, Class: Cephalopoda, Order: Ammonitida,
Family: Pachydiscidae, and Genus: Lewesiceras.

Lewesiceras is a genus of large ammonites belonging to the order Ammonitida and the family Pachydiscidae.
They lived in the late Cretaceous period, in the Cenomanian and Turonian ages,
which occurred 99.6-89.3 million years ago.
These shelled ammonoids were nektonic, fast-moving and carnivorous.
Fossils distribution:
Cretaceous of Armenia, France, Germany, United States (Texas), Uzbekistan.

Species:
Lewesiceras mantelli Wright and Wright, 1951.
Lewesiceras peramplum (Mantell, 1822).
Lewesiceras wiedmanni Wright & Kennedy, 1984.

The ammonite species originally described as Lewesiceras anapadense by Kossmat is now generally referred
to by its accepted taxonomic name, Menabonites anapadensis (Kossmat, 1898).
The species name refers to the Anaipadi Formation in the Cauvery Basin of South India where it was found.
Classification and Details:
Genus: Menabonites
Species: anapadensis
Authority: (Kossmat, 1898) (the parentheses indicate the species was originally placed in a different genus)
Geological Age: The species is characteristic of the lower Coniacian stage of the Late Cretaceous period.
Location: Found in the Cauvery Basin, Tamil Nadu, south-east India, specifically within the Anaipadi Formation.

Paleoecology: Like other ammonites, this species was a nektonic (free-swimming), fast-moving carnivore that
lived in the ancient marine environments of the Cretaceous.
While some sources still refer to it by the older name in casual mentions or older literature,
current systematic paleontology classifies it under the genus Menabonites.
